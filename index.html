About

At UBC MIST, our team strives to augment the UBC biomedical engineering student community's capacity to participate in, and contribute to, the larger conversation on healthcare as well as the biomedical technology in it. Our central approach to achieving this is through participation in biomedical engineering competitions around the world, where we put forward new solutions to pressing medical challenges and help build the technical abilities of our team members.

2018/19

Collaborating with the Rick Hansen Institute, our team has learned about the importance of power assist devices for manual wheelchairs. Manual propelling a wheelchair for long stretches of time can lead to shoulder injuries. Additionally, some patients with Spinal Cord Injury suffer impaired hand functions, thus cannot properly propel wheelchairs forward. However, these devices can cost upwards of $10,000 and can be a financial burden for many. As a result, our team will be focusing our 2019/2020 project on an affordable wheelchair power assist device for developing countries.

Project Beacon

With our team's model being driven by a new innovation every year, MIST centered the 2018/19 project around mental health. While researching projects for the team, we realized that mental health is a key topic in medicine but is not given as much attention in engineering. This was the catalyst that sparked our interest in the topic, as we believe that there is a lot of room for innovation and growth in this particular field.

Our app, Beacon, is connected to a wearable device which tracks the user’s biometric data. Our machine learning technology creates a unique user profile which allows the user to gain insight into their stress levels throughout daily life.

Project Beacon is for those who aren't sure if they need help yet or know they do but are scared to go get the help. It serves as a stepping stone to getting the proper help, as well as a companion to keep track of your anxiety and mood throughout the months, weeks and days At the end of the day, it helps you stay in touch with your mental state and manage it.
